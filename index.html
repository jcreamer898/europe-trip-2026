🇫🇷 Paris

5 Nights · 1st / 2nd / 3rd Arrondissement · 2-Bedroom Airbnb

View from Sacré-Cœur — Photo: Wikimedia Commons

Day 1 — Arrival in Paris

Travel + Settle In

Getting There

Overnight flight from Nashville (BNA) to Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG). Arrive in the morning and ease into the city.

🚆 RER B → Châtelet-Les Halles
~35 minutes, ~€11.50/adult. Children under 10 ride half fare. Buy tickets at the station or use the Île-de-France Mobilités app.

Afternoon

  • Check into your Airbnb, drop bags, and take a deep breath — you're in Paris!
  • Grab croissants and café crème at a nearby boulangerie
  • Stock up on basics at Franprix or Monoprix for breakfasts and snacks
  • Explore your neighborhood on foot — the Montorgueil area (2nd arr.) is perfect for this
🛝 Jardin Nelson Mandela at Les Halles
Trampolines, tube slides, fully fenced. Perfect for burning off plane energy while the adults sip coffee nearby.

Evening

Early dinner at a neighborhood bistro — the girls will be fading from jet lag, and that's okay. Grab glaces (ice cream) on the walk home.

Day 2 — The Classics, Reimagined

Tuileries · Notre-Dame · Luxembourg

Morning

  • Walk to Tuileries Garden — one of the world's most beautiful urban parks
  • Hit the Tuileries playground: trampolines, slides, and a classic carousel
  • Stroll along the Seine toward Île de la Cité

Midday

  • Notre-Dame Cathedral exterior — see the restoration progress up close
  • Browse the flower market on Île de la Cité
  • Lunch: Crêpes near Saint-Michel (the girls will love picking fillings)

Afternoon

🛝 Luxembourg Gardens — Le Ludo Jardin
This is a highlight. Zipline, climbing structures, sandbox, and a puppet theater (Théâtre du Luxembourg). Rent toy sailboats to push across the pond (~€5). Playground entry ~€3/child.

Evening

Wander the streets of Saint-Germain-des-Prés — grab dinner at a local café with terrace seating.

Day 3 — Montmartre & Local Life

Sacré-Cœur · Buttes-Chaumont · Canal Saint-Martin

Morning

  • Métro to Montmartre — take the stairs up (more fun than the funicular!)
  • Sacré-Cœur Basilica — the views from the steps are breathtaking
  • Artists' quarter at Place du Tertre — maybe get a family caricature?
  • The "I Love You" wall at Abbesses — "I love you" in 250 languages

Lunch

Café terrace in Montmartre — croque monsieur, french onion soup, and citron pressé for the girls.

Afternoon — Choose Your Own Adventure

🛝 Option A: Parc des Buttes-Chaumont
Paris's wildest park. Caves, a waterfall, a temple on a cliff over a lake, and an adventure playground. The girls will think it's an enchanted forest.
🛝 Option B: Champ de Mars Playground
Right under the Eiffel Tower. Great playground with tower views. Perfect if you want to see the tower without doing the tourist queue.

Evening

Walk along Canal Saint-Martin — iron footbridges, leafy banks, very local. Dinner in the 10th arrondissement.

🎠 Disneyland Paris

2 Days of Magic · RER A from Central Paris

Sleeping Beauty Castle, Disneyland Paris — Photo: Wikimedia Commons

Day 4 — Disneyland Park

Main Park Day
🚆 RER A → Marne-la-Vallée / Chessy
~40 minutes from Châtelet. ~€8-10/person each way. Covered by Navigo weekly pass (all zones).

Must-Do Rides

  • Big Thunder Mountain — the whole family will love this one
  • Pirates of the Caribbean — a classic
  • Hyperspace Mountain — for the 11-year-old thrill seeker
  • Buzz Lightyear Laser Blast — competitive family fun
  • It's a Small World — charming European version
  • Peter Pan's Flight — get in line early, always popular
💡 Pro Tip
Download the Disneyland Paris app for wait times. Hit Fantasyland first thing (Peter Pan, Small World) before lines build. Save Frontierland and Adventureland for afternoon.

Evening

Stay for the parade and the evening show — Disney Illuminations is spectacular. The castle lighting is worth the wait.

Day 5 — Walt Disney Studios / Disney Adventure World

Studios + Frozen!

Morning — Disney Adventure World

  • World of Frozen — opened March 2026! Brand new Frozen-themed land
  • Ratatouille: The Adventure — shrink to rat-size in Gusteau's kitchen
  • Avengers Campus — meet characters, ride Spider-Man W.E.B. Adventure
💡 World of Frozen
This just opened and will be hugely popular. Arrive at park opening and head straight there. Everly (7) is going to lose her mind.

Afternoon

Hop back to the main Disneyland Park to revisit favorites or catch anything you missed yesterday.

Evening

Dinner at Disney Village (Earl of Sandwich is a solid family choice) before the RER back to Paris. The girls will sleep on the train.

🇧🇪 Belgium

2 Nights in Ghent · Day Trip to Bruges · Hotel near Korenmarkt

Graslei Waterfront, Ghent — Photo: Wikimedia Commons

Day 6 — Paris to Ghent

Travel Day

Morning

Pack up, say au revoir to Paris. One last pain au chocolat from the boulangerie downstairs.

🚄 Eurostar: Paris Gare du Nord → Brussels-Midi
~1 hour 20 minutes. Book early for best fares (€40-80 vs €150+ last minute).
🚆 IC Train: Brussels → Ghent-Sint-Pieters
~30 minutes. No reservation needed. Belgian rail is cheap and easy.

Afternoon — Explore Ghent

  • Check into your hotel near Korenmarkt
  • Walk the Graslei & Korenlei waterfront — the most photographed spot in Ghent
  • St. Bavo's Cathedral — home of the famous Ghent Altarpiece
  • Gravensteen Castle exterior — a medieval castle right in the city center

Evening

Belgian frites from a frituur (Hadley and Everly will be in heaven), waffles for dessert, and hot chocolate or Belgian beer by the canal.

💡 Hotel vs. Airbnb
For just 2 nights, a hotel is the better call. You dodge the Airbnb cleaning fee ($75-150), get daily housekeeping, and have a reception desk for local tips. Look for family rooms near Korenmarkt.

Day 7 — Bruges Day Trip + Ghent

Two Cities, One Day
Rozenhoedkaai, Bruges — Photo: Wikimedia Commons

Morning — Bruges

🚆 Train: Ghent → Bruges
~30 minutes. Cheap Belgian rail, no reservation needed. Runs frequently.
  • Markt Square — the stunning medieval main square
  • Belfry of Bruges — climb the 366 steps for incredible views (if the girls are up for it!)
  • Canal boat tour — ~30 min, ~€10/person. The best way to see Bruges
  • Chocolate shops — this is Belgium, after all. The Chocolate Line is excellent
🛝 Koningin Astridpark, Bruges
Right near the city center. A nice green space with playground equipment where the girls can run around between sightseeing stops.

Afternoon — Back to Ghent

  • Train back to Ghent (~30 min)
  • Explore the Patershol neighborhood — cobblestone streets, cozy restaurants
  • Hunt for Ghent street art — the city is covered in incredible murals
  • Belgian craft beer tasting for the adults (Gruut brewery is local)
🛝 Citadelpark, Ghent
Large park with playground, pond, and green space. Also check out Blaarmeersen recreation park if you want something bigger.

Day 8 — Ghent to London

Travel Day
🚆 IC Train: Ghent → Brussels-Midi (~30 min)
🚄 Eurostar: Brussels → London St Pancras (~2 hours)
Total journey ~3 hours including transfer. Book early for the best Eurostar fares.

Afternoon

  • Tube from St Pancras to Notting Hill Gate or Paddington
  • Check into your Airbnb — you know this neighborhood. Welcome home (again)!
🛝 Holland Park Adventure Playground
Towers, zip wire, climbing frames. And the park has resident peacocks — Everly will freak out. This is 10 minutes from your Airbnb.

Evening

Walk Portobello Road, soak in the neighborhood you love. Local pub dinner — Sunday roast if timing works out.

Days 9–12 — London, Like Locals

Your 4th Visit · Skip the Tourist Traps

You know London. You love London. This isn't about ticking boxes — it's about living here for a few days. Here's a menu of ideas to mix and match:

🏘 Neighborhoods & Markets

  • Portobello Road Market — Saturday is the biggest day (antiques, food, vintage)
  • Borough Market — incredible lunch spot. Scotch eggs, raclette, every cuisine imaginable
  • Columbia Road Flower Market — Sunday mornings only. Gorgeous.
  • Camden Market — reach it by walking the canal from Little Venice (see below)

🚴 Walks & Green Spaces

  • Hyde Park long walks — Serpentine lake, Italian Gardens, right out your door
  • Regent's Canal: Little Venice → Camden — one of London's best walks. Narrowboats, cafés, street art, 45 minutes end to end
  • Hampstead Heath — Parliament Hill for the best views of the London skyline
  • South Bank walk — Tate Modern → Shakespeare's Globe → Borough Market

🎭 Culture

  • West End show — book a matinee. Hadley (11) is old enough for most shows. Check TKTS booth in Leicester Square for day-of discounts
  • Natural History Museum — free, always worth a revisit. The Hintze Hall blue whale never gets old
  • V&A Museum — free. Fashion, design, and a beautiful café
  • Science Museum — free. Wonderlab is great for kids (~£10)
🛝 Coram's Fields
7 acres of playground. The rule: adults are only admitted if accompanied by a child. City farm, sandpits, paddling pool (summer). A true hidden gem.
🛝 Jubilee Gardens Playground (South Bank)
Right by the London Eye. Great play structures, sand & water play. Perfect mid-walk stop on the South Bank.
🛝 Holland Park Adventure Playground
You'll probably come back here multiple times. It's that good, and it's that close to home base.
💡 Diana Memorial Playground
Heads up: Diana Memorial Playground in Kensington Gardens is closed for refurbishment until summer 2026. Check before visiting.
💳 London Transit
Under-11s ride free on all TfL (Tube, bus, Overground). Adults: just tap contactless — daily cap is £8.90 for Zones 1-2. No Oyster card needed.

Day 13 — Fly Home

London → Nashville

Morning

  • Pack up, say goodbye to London (for now — see you next year?)
  • Full English breakfast at a local café to fuel the journey
🚆 Getting to Heathrow
Piccadilly Line from Paddington area: ~50 min, cheapest option (~£5.50/adult).
Heathrow Express from Paddington: 15 min, premium option (~£25/adult).
Elizabeth Line from Paddington: ~30 min, great middle ground (~£12.80).

Flight

LHR → BNA. British Airways flies direct 3x/week (check schedule). Otherwise Delta via ATL or JFK. You'll land in Nashville the same day you left.

📅 Booking Strategy

When to book what — and how to save the most

✈ Flights

Open-jaw ticket: BNA → CDG, LHR → BNA. Search on Google Flights + Skyscanner. Best search days: Sunday, Tuesday, Wednesday. BA flies BNA→LHR direct 3x/week.

Book 3–5 months ahead (5–6 for summer)

🏠 Paris Airbnb

2-bedroom in 1st/2nd/3rd arr. The Montorgueil neighborhood (2nd) is ideal: walkable, local bakeries, near Les Halles and Métro. Filter for Superhost, washer, and good reviews from families.

Book 3–4 months ahead

🏨 Ghent Hotel

Family room near Korenmarkt. 2 nights doesn't justify an Airbnb cleaning fee ($75-150). Hotels give you flexibility and daily housekeeping.

Book 2–3 months ahead

🏠 London Airbnb

2-bedroom in Notting Hill / Paddington / Bayswater. Just north of Hyde Park. You know the area — aim for the streets you love. Superhost, washer, walkable to Tube.

Book 3–4 months ahead

🚄 Eurostar

Paris → Brussels + Brussels → London. Tickets release up to 4 months ahead. Early booking saves 60-70%. Set an alert on the Eurostar app for your dates.

Book at ticket release

🎠 Disneyland Paris

Date-tiered pricing: Eco/Low dates are cheapest. Weekdays in shoulder season are 30-40% cheaper than peak. 2-day/2-park tickets give the best value. Buy on the official site — not resellers.

Book 2–3 months ahead
💡 Best Travel Windows
Late September / early October or April / May are the sweet spot: mild weather, smaller crowds, shoulder-season pricing. Summer (June–August) is most expensive but aligns with school schedules. If you can swing September, you'll save 20-30% across the board.

💡 Paris Transit Hack
Navigo Weekly Pass: €32.40/person — unlimited Metro, RER (all zones, including CDG airport and Disneyland), buses, and trams for a full week. The 7-year-old rides free; the 11-year-old needs a child Navigo (half fare). This is almost certainly cheaper than buying individual tickets. The week runs Monday–Sunday, so plan arrival day accordingly.
